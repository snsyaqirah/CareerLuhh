Why This Exists

The Problem

Malaysia produces 250,000 graduates per year. More than half will spend their first 6–12 months underemployed or unemployed — not because they lack ability, but because the systems between them and the right employer were never designed to see the whole picture. Candidates apply to 50 jobs and hear back from three. Employers post a role, get 400 applications, and hire the one whose CV happened to match a filter. Both sides end up cynical. Neither got what they actually needed.

No Map

Career counselling is scarce, expensive, or generic. Students graduate without knowing what roles match their trajectory — only what keywords to paste into a CV.

No Signal

Job boards rank by recency or sponsored placement. Candidates can't see their fit, their gap, or what to do about it. TVET graduates and self-taught talent are invisible to keyword filters.

No Co-Pilot

Employers hire reactively. By the time a resignation lands, the signal was visible weeks earlier — if anyone was watching.

02

Our Answer

What CareerLuhh Does

A three-portal career operating system built around one continuous career profile that travels with the user — from student, to first job, to senior pivot, across every phase of working life.

It does not predict the future. It shows the realistic range of paths available to someone with a specific shape: their skills, their qualifications, their location, their goals. Every recommendation is explainable in one plain sentence. No black-box scores. No false precision.

🎓 Student Portal
  • Readiness score 0–100 with gap breakdown
  • 3-path career roadmap year by year
  • Internship matcher by absorption rate

The Engine

16 Specialised AI Agents

⚡

Each agent does one clearly scoped job. They trigger on explicit user intent only — not on page load, not simultaneously. Each passes a shared SessionContext forward so every recommendation knows what the previous one established. No agent calls another directly — the orchestrator connects them.

2The NavigatorNavigatorAgentStudentMaps 3 realistic career paths with year-by-year milestones

4The TranslatorSkorAlignAgentStudent CandidateTranslates Malaysian qualifications to global equivalents employers understand

Impact

SDG Alignment

SDG 4 · Quality Education

Career path guidance + skills gap analysis helps every Malaysian make better education decisions — not just those with access to good counsellors.

SDG 5 · Gender Equality

Blind recruitment mode removes name, gender, photo, and university from screening — candidates evaluated on competency alone.

SDG 8 · Decent Work

Salary benchmarking, gig-work credentialing, job board without a top-5 degree. TVET graduates are first-class users — not edge cases.

SDG 10 · Reduced Inequalities

Lokal Route shows real net take-home KL vs hometown. Rural youth are not fooled by big-city salary numbers that disappear into rent.
